Smart Assignment

An agentic workflow that recommends the best delivery day & time slot for a new customer — enforcing hard operational rules in code, ranking the survivors on weighted business factors, and escalating to a human when the call is close.

✅ Deterministic, auditable decisions 🤖 LLM-written reasoning 🧪 Running on mock data
The problem

Onboarding a new account, every time, the same way

When a new foodservice customer signs on, someone has to decide which delivery route and time window they should join. That decision balances truck capacity, geography, and the customer's preference — and it needs to be consistent, explainable, and fast. Smart Assignment makes that call automatically, and flags the tricky ones for a specialist instead of guessing.

How it works

Five steps, from intake to recommendation

The customer's details go in one end; a fully-explained slot recommendation (or a human escalation) comes out the other.

1

Intake

Capture the customer's address, order quantity (cases), and preferred delivery window (optional).

2

Geo-Lookup

Geocode the address and pick the Top-N nearest candidate routes by proximity.

3

Constraint Check

Drop any route that fails a hard rule — serviceability, capacity, or window.

4

Score & Rank

Rank the survivors with weighted scoring across clustering, capacity buffer, and window fit.

5

Recommend

Return the top slot with a reasoning trace — or escalate to a human specialist.

The rules it enforces

Hard constraints — non-negotiable, checked in code

These are objective facts, not judgment calls. A route that fails any of them is removed before ranking — the agent can never "reason" a customer onto a full truck or outside the serviceable area.

📍

Geographic serviceability

The customer must fall within the route's serviceable radius.

📦

Route capacity

The truck stays at or below 90% capacity after adding this order.

🕑

Delivery-window fit

The route offers a window overlapping the customer's preference, if stated.

How it ranks the rest

Weighted scoring factors

Among the routes that pass every hard rule, these weighted factors decide the winner. Weights reflect priority and are fully configurable.

🧭

Geographic clustering

How tightly the customer clusters with the route's existing stops — tighter clusters mean more predictable arrivals.

weight 0.45
🛡️

Capacity buffer

How much headroom is left after the add — more buffer means a route that's resilient to day-of disruption.

weight 0.30
🎯

Window match

How well the route's window covers the customer's stated preference.

weight 0.25
The outcome

Three possible decisions

Every run ends in one of three states. Anything below a 70% confidence threshold, or with no valid slot at all, goes to a human — with full context attached.

✔ Recommended

A clear winner above the confidence threshold — ready to auto-assign.

⚠ Low confidence

A slot is proposed, but the options are close — a specialist confirms before committing.

✖ No feasible slot

Every candidate failed a hard rule — routed to a specialist for a manual decision.
